Who competes in WSL CT?: The seaosn starts with 36 Surfers: 32 qualified surfers, 2 season wildcards, and 2 event wildcards Mid-Season cut-off: The field is reduced to the top 22 surfers + 2 event wildcards fro each event Finals: Finals consist of the top 5 surfers rankked by total points, and they compete in a 1 day tournment where the winner is crowned the Champion *** 6 of 11 United States of America Surfers are from Hawaii resulting in Brazil technically having the most surfers followed by Australia, then Hawaii How the points system works? Each event is styled as a bracketed competion with the final few round consisting of head to head match-ups. In a heat Surfers are compete based off the combined score of their top 2 waves which are judged from 0-10 Judging scale: [0.0 — 1.9: Poor] [2.0 — 4.9: Fair] [5.0 — 6.4: Good] [6.5 — 7.9: Very Good] [8.0 — 10.0: Excellent] Surfers can ride up to 10 waves and must abide to priorty and interference rules or points get deducted from their score.

The Final 5: After 11 events the top 5 surfers based off total points face off for the world title The tournement is structured so the number 1 ranked surfer only has to win heat to be crowned the champion Match 1 Rank #5 vs Rank Elimination Match 2 Winner of Match 1 vs Rank #3 Elimination Match 3 Winner of Match 2 vs Rank #2 Elimination Title Match Winner of Match 3 vs Rank #1 Best of 3 heats So what happened?
